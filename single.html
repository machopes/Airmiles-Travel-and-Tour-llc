Copacabana, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

Famed for its crescent-shaped beach, Copacabana is one of Rio’s liveliest neighborhoods, drawing a cross-section of locals and visitors to round-the-clock activity along its sands and mosaic-tiled promenade. Amid the high-rises overlooking the beach are the art deco Copacabana Palace hotel and imposing Copacabana Fort, home to a military museum. Inland, laid-back sidewalk cafes and casual bars line tranquil streets.

Copacabana beach, located at the Atlantic shore, stretches from Posto Dois (lifeguard watchtower Two) to Posto Seis (lifeguard watchtower Six). Leme is at Posto Um (lifeguard watchtower One). There are historic forts at both ends of Copacabana beach; Fort Copacabana, built in 1914, is at the south end by Posto Seis and Fort Duque de Caxias, built in 1779, at the north end. Hotels, restaurants, bars, nightclubs and residential buildings dot the promenade facing Avenida Atlântica. On Sundays and holidays, one side of Avenida Atlântica is closed to cars, giving residents and tourists more space for activities along the beach. Copacabana Beach plays host to millions of revellers during the annual New Year's Eve celebrations, and in most years, has been the official venue of the FIFA Beach Soccer World Cup.

Victoria Falls, Zambia

Victoria Falls is a waterfall on the Zambezi River in southern Africa, which provides habitat for several unique species of plants and animals. It is located on the border between Zambia and Zimbabwe and is one of the world's largest waterfalls, with a width of 1,708 m.

Saud Beach, Luzon, Philippines

Saud Beach is more popularly known as “Pagudpud Beach.” The picturesque 2-kilometer white sand beach is often featured in travel photos of Ilocos Norte, and is known for its pristine waters and beautiful coast lined with coconut palms.Saud Beach has cream-colored sand and cool waters, perfect for swimming and beach bumming in. Have a relaxing day or afternoon just on the beach. There is plenty of room for activities such as volleyball or throwing a Frisbee

One of the nicest beaches visited in Luzon with light sand and clear waters. I’m from Australia, and came to this place with my family in two full vans with small children. We stayed in Fort Ilocandia Laoag city and travelled 2 hrs to the beach. The water gets pretty deep after about 2 meters from the shore (but maybe it was high tide as we were there around afternoon) so perhaps it is not too child friendly. The water was calm and would’ve been perfect to use SUP if I had remembered to bring it. The water was clear and had some rocks, corals(?) so it was fun to go snorkeling and could see some tropical fish. Word of warning, my sister and I saw a couple of the black and White Sea snake amongst the rocks so it is highly advisable not to walk on rocks, wear swimming shoes, and only venture out to the deeper areas if you are experienced swimmer and just avoid stepping on the rocks. The beach was one of the cleanest beaches I’ve been to in the Philippines. It has cottages for rent and at the time of visit, the shower rooms and toilets were being renovated but there was one outdoor shower near the entrance. There is an entrance fee, it was around ₽20 or $1 if you’re a foreigner. Prices may change but know there is an entrance fee. There was a volleyball net if you need a break from the water and boats were also seen near the shore (maybe you could hire them?). Overall, had a good time at this beach, not too child friendly, clear blue waters, clean light sand, and would definitely visit this place again.
